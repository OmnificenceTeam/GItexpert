The proportion of patients who had an objective response was chosen as the primary endpoint because no data on median overall survival for FOLFIRI in combination with either bevacizumab or cetuximab in the first-line treatment of metastatic colorectal cancer were available at the time of the study design.

Of the 752 randomly assigned subjects 380 were in the FOLFIRI plus cetuximab group and 372 were assigned to the FOLFIRI plus bevacizumab group. The ITT population in the groups was 297 and 295 respectively

Page 4 Question

The baseline characteristics of the treatment groups were similar (table 1). With more exclusions in the FOLFIRI plus cetuximab group, the per-protocol population of assessable patients consisted of 526 patients; 255 in the cetuximab group and 271 in the bevacizumab group.

In the primary analysis, complete or partial responses were reported in 184 (62%) of 297 patients in the cetuximab group and 171 (58%) of 295 patients in the bevacizumab group (OR 1.18, 95% CI 0.85–1.64; p=0.18; table 2). The proportion of patients who achieved a complete response was higher in the cetuximab than in the bevacizumab group (table 2). In the predefined per protocol population, the proportion of patients who achieved a response was signifi cantly higher in the cetuximab group than in the bevacizumab group (184 [72%] of 255 vs 171 [63%] of 271, respectively; p=0.017).
